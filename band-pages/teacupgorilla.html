Teacup Gorilla

I joined Teacup Gorilla in the fall of 2017 after being a fan for several years. The band had been know for their mixture of Americana's warmth mixed with the dynamic shift and moodiness of post-rock. Often compared to The Velvet Underground, Explosions in the Sky, and early Modest Mouse, I was brought in for my ability to create texture, as well as my rhythm guitar ability. Little did they know they'd get a sometimes lead guitarist and riff creator in the process.
‍
Teacup Gorilla is Dan Eisenstat - Guitar & Vocals, Sondra Eby - Drums & Vocals, Miriam Suzanne - Vocals & Bass, Josie Cool - Guitar & Vocals

"America's Next Top Waffle" is a song I wrote write as I was coming out as a trans woman. The small terrier that my partner and I had kept sense she was a puppy had gotten really sick and we took her in to the vet for care. Turns out she had pancreatitis. I was allowed to visit her at 9pm after she had been under doctor care for about 12 hours, so I went in to say goodnight and give her some pets. She was barely awake and I was worried she wouldn't make it through the night. As I came out of the vet, I looked up at the moon shining in the cool november sky and it was a day or two from being a new moon, just a sliver of white in the black sky. The majority of the first verse came out in sobs in the vet parking lot.

This version was recorded at 7th Circle Music Collective in Denver Colorado in early 2018 and was filmed, recorded, and edited by my wonderful friends at 7th Circle Sessions.

This is a live, audience shot video of us performing a song called "Whiskey from Strangers, Part 2". It's part of a 20 minute long suite of music we've been recording since fall of 2019, and that the other 3 members have been working on for over 5 years. This suite, as well as most of the cd-ep the band released before I joined share a common thread of telling the take of Herman and Sam - a modern telling of the greek story of Salmacis and Hermaphroditus. The music is canon to Miriam's open source novel Riding Side Saddle

In 2015, before I joined, the band released a CD-ep entitled The Holes They Leave, and it's available on all streaming services, or you can purchase it from the band on Bandcamp
